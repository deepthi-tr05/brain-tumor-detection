What We Detect

4 Tumor Classifications

Our model is trained to classify MRI scans into one of four categories with confidence scoring and stage analysis.

✅
No Tumor
Normal

MRI scan appears normal with no detectable abnormal growth or lesion. Routine follow-up scans are still recommended.

Characteristics
No visible mass or lesion
Normal brain structure
Uniform tissue density
🧬
Pituitary Tumor
Pituitary Adenoma

Arises from the pituitary gland at the brain's base. Most are benign (adenomas) but can cause hormonal imbalances and vision issues.

Common Symptoms
Vision changes / headaches
Hormonal imbalances
Unusual thirst / urination
🔬
Meningioma Tumor
Meningeal Origin

Forms on the membranes (meninges) surrounding the brain. Usually slow-growing and often benign, but location can affect treatment.

Common Symptoms
Headaches, seizures
Arm / leg weakness
Speech difficulties
⚠️
Glioma Tumor
Glial Cell Origin

Originates in glial cells of the brain/spinal cord. Can be low-grade (slow) to high-grade (aggressive GBM). Requires urgent evaluation.

Common Symptoms
Severe sudden headaches
Personality/memory changes
Seizures, nausea
The Process

How NeuroScan AI Works

1. Upload MRI

Drag & drop your brain MRI scan (JPG/PNG). Fill in basic patient information.

2. AI Preprocessing

Image is resized to 224×224, normalized, and fed into the VGG16 model pipeline.

3. Deep Learning

VGG16 transfer-learned CNN classifies the scan into one of 4 tumor categories.

4. Heatmap + 3D

A heatmap overlay and interactive 3D surface model shows the suspected tumor region.

5. PDF Report

Full diagnostic report with stage analysis, precautions, and MRI image — downloadable instantly.
